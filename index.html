RGB interpolation draws a straight line through the color cube, often passing through desaturated (muddy) colors in the middle.

HSV interpolation travels around the color wheel, keeping saturation and brightness consistent — resulting in more vibrant transitions.

About

RGB and Hue interpolation are two ways to blend between colors. RGB takes a straight line through the color cube — fast but often muddy. Hue interpolation travels around the color wheel, preserving saturation for vibrant gradients.
